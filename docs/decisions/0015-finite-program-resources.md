# 0015 — Finite program resources

## Decision

Programs cannot recruit, retain staff, heal players or improve facilities through abstract prestige alone. They receive finite budgets and capacity, and every institutional action consumes or depends on those resources.

## Consequences

- Prestige remains an advantage but is not a guarantee.
- A successful small program can expand through donor confidence.
- A wealthy program can become inefficient or financially pressured.
- Recruiting and transfer decisions compete for the same limited market capacity.
- Medical and development quality emerge from institutional investment.
- The world can produce financial rises, stagnation and collapses without hero-specific events.
