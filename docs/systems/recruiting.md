# College recruiting

## Scope

The recruiting module models evaluation during the final high-school season. It does not grant offers from a single global popularity score.

## Program ecosystem

Each career creates 24 fictional college programs from the world seed. Every program keeps its own:

- prestige and conference level;
- offensive or defensive scheme;
- academic threshold;
- medical and training quality;
- need at the hero's position;
- depth-chart competition;
- youth opportunity;
- scouting confidence;
- interest and current recruiting stage.

Only the most relevant programs are shown in the main interface. The rest remain active in the background.

## Evaluation inputs

Programs reconsider the athlete after matches using:

- game film;
- consistency across completed games;
- level of opposition;
- athletic and technical profile;
- health and current pain;
- GPA and attendance;
- recommendation from the school staff;
- scheme fit;
- current positional need.

Interest and scouting confidence are separate. A program can like the profile while still lacking enough reliable film to contact the athlete.

## Stages

`unaware -> watchlist -> evaluating -> contact -> priority -> offered`

A program can cool its interest when the profile no longer fits its needs.

## Player agency

The player receives two recruiting actions per season week:

- send film;
- ask the school coach to call;
- send the academic transcript;
- declare serious interest.

Actions improve information or relationships with a program. They never guarantee an offer.

## Offers

A full scholarship requires high interest, sufficient scouting confidence, academic eligibility, acceptable health and actual need at the position. The projected role is stored separately from program prestige.
