# World Constitution — v1

World Constitution is the rules layer beneath the football ecosystem. It prevents recruiting, eligibility, roster movement and season progression from inventing their own clocks or limits.

## Calendar

The world uses one annual cycle:

1. summer recruiting;
2. preseason;
3. regular season;
4. postseason;
5. winter evaluation and movement;
6. spring development.

Season rollover happens in the next preseason, not immediately after the conference final. Offseason movement is processed during winter evaluation.

## Eligibility

PROSPECT models the real transition period rather than rewriting every athlete under one rule:

- college athletes who enrolled before 2027 keep the legacy four-seasons-in-five framework and the limited-participation redshirt path;
- prospects first enrolling from fall 2027 use the continuous age-based five-year window;
- high-school eligibility remains separate;
- athletic availability also requires academic eligibility.

The hero reaches college in 2027 and therefore enters the modern five-year model.

## Academic progress

College players track:

- current-term credits;
- degree progress;
- academic standing;
- athletic eligibility.

Programs have different academic-support quality. Falling behind can remove a player from the active depth chart without deleting him from the roster.

## Rosters and scholarships

College programs operate under:

- a hard roster limit;
- a scholarship limit;
- a funded-scholarship budget that may be lower than the legal maximum;
- an estimated full roster alongside the detailed active-position simulation;
- compliance states: clear, warning and violation.

Recruiting and transfer destinations must have real roster room. High-level recruits also require funded aid space.

## Simulation order

Every world step follows the same order:

1. calendar;
2. eligibility;
3. rosters;
4. teams;
5. competition;
6. recruiting;
7. movement;
8. history.

UI and career events may display these changes but cannot bypass them.
