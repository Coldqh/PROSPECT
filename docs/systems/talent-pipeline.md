# Annual talent pipeline

The talent pipeline keeps the football world supplied with new players without recycling old entities or spawning emergency recruits around the hero.

## Regional production

Every prospect belongs to a persistent region. Regions define football culture, population, infrastructure, scouting exposure and academic access. These values affect the size and shape of each annual class but never hard-lock an individual outcome.

## Development profiles

High-school players enter through traditional, multi-sport or late-bloomer profiles. The profile changes physical maturity, development timing and how quickly a scouting grade becomes reliable.

## Camps and discovery

Spring combines and summer showcases occur on the world calendar. Only players who can realistically attend are evaluated. Camp performance changes verified scouting information and exposure; it does not grant arbitrary permanent bonuses.

## Alternative routes

Unsigned seniors do not simply disappear. Eligible players may continue through a JUCO or walk-on route, develop outside the main recruiting cycle, receive later interest and eventually enter a college roster.

## Annual flow

During winter evaluation the world:

1. closes eligibility and enrollment decisions;
2. preserves unsigned players with viable alternative routes;
3. advances existing independent prospects;
4. enrolls committed JUCO and walk-on players;
5. generates a freshman class for each active high-school team;
6. refreshes roster limits and class history.

All generation and movement is deterministic for the same world seed and season year.
