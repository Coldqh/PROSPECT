# Multi-year roster management

Every active football program owns a three-year roster plan. The plan is generated from the real roster, eligibility windows, projected departures, scholarships, transfer risk, coaching pressure, team expectations and available resources.

## Position projection

For QB, RB, WR, LB and CB the program stores:

- current detailed depth;
- players expected back next season;
- players expected back in two seasons;
- projected departures;
- scholarship count;
- average and best overall;
- average potential;
- current and future need;
- target additions for the next class.

## Program strategy

A program chooses one of four strategies:

- contend — protect immediate results and recruit ready players;
- balanced — maintain current quality without overloading classes;
- develop — preserve young talent and invest in long-term growth;
- rebuild — accept short-term weakness to replace an aging roster.

## Player usage

Every player receives one active usage plan:

- starter;
- rotation;
- special teams;
- developmental;
- redshirt for eligible legacy players.

The assignment affects game participation and whether a legacy season of eligibility is preserved.

## Offseason decisions

During winter evaluation the staff can:

- award available scholarship aid;
- move a buried athlete to a position with future need;
- mark young players for redshirt or developmental use;
- reserve roster and scholarship space for the incoming class.

After transfers, enrollment and the coaching carousel the plan is recalculated. A new staff is not forced to keep the previous staff's roster assumptions.
